Link Best Practices

As with all code on your page, how you write it affects whether or not your page is usable and accessible.

  • The text inside of your link should tell the user what will happen when they click on it
    • Link text should never say something like "click here"
    • Bad: Click here to submit our form
    • Good: You can contact us through our form
    • Example dummy link: Learn more
  • Test your links from every page
  • Use the correct type of link when adding one to your page

About Links

You will be learning about and creating six types of hyperlinks:

  • absolute
  • relative
  • fragment
  • email
  • telephone
  • relative with fragment

Absolute Hyperlinks

Absolute hyperlinks are used when linking to another website. For example, visit ASU.edu.

Relative Hyperlinks

Relative hyperlinks are used when linking to a page within your own website.
